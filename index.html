01

A bit about me

I'm a software engineering student who spends most of his energy thinking about games — the kind of person who enjoys both clean architecture and the dumb thrill of finally fixing a physics bug at 2 a.m.

Games are where it all clicks for me. I like working close to the metal: how a mechanic reads to the player, why a frame stutters, what makes a world feel lived-in instead of just rendered. UE5 is my main playground.

Outside of that you'll catch me breaking apart shaders, sketching procedural systems, or reading way too much about how old games pulled off impossible things on tiny hardware.
